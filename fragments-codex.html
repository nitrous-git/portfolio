Game overview

Broken time, fractured worlds, combat realms

The fiction follows the public game pitch closely: time is broken, the worlds have splintered, and the player crosses ruined realities in search of the lost Fragments of the CodeX. The experience starts with atmosphere and mystery, then pushes the player into traversal, world transitions, and boss-driven combat spaces where reality itself feels unstable.

Framework and systems

Technical side of the production

Fragments of the CodeX is where the underlying framework work becomes visible. The goal is not to hard-script one polished boss fight and stop there. The goal is to build a reusable gameplay spine that can support multiple bosses, traversal devices, encounter types, world transitions, and future slice expansion without collapsing into one-off Blueprint logic.

  • Blueprint component-based framework: systems are split into reusable modules instead of being buried inside one giant character or boss Blueprint.
  • Stack-based FSM architecture: boss behavior and encounter flow are driven by layered, state-oriented logic that is easier to extend and reason about than ad hoc scripting.
  • CodeX combat orchestration layer: shared combat structure, boss sequencing, timing, and behavioral control are treated as first-class systems.
  • Traversal and transition pipeline: teleporter logic, reveal effects, world transitions, and section-to-section flow are part of the same authored experience.
  • World Partition and streamed sections: large-scale level structure supports the feeling of crossing broken spaces without flattening everything into one arena.
  • Vertical slice validation: every feature has to work both as a player-facing moment and as proof that the framework can scale.
